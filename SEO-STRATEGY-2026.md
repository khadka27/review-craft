## Best Practices for Review Mockups in Design

### 1. Match Platform Styling Exactly

Use review mockups that accurately replicate each platform's design. Inaccurate mockups will
look amateurish in client presentations or portfolios.

Verify:
- Font styles and sizes match the real platform
- Star icons are platform-accurate
- Badge designs (verified, top reviewer, etc.) are correct
- Colors match (platform-specific blue, gray, etc.)

### 2. Create Varied Reviews

Don't use the same review repeatedly. Create variety:

- Different reviewer names and avatars
- Mix of 3, 4, and 5-star reviews
- Various review lengths (short vs. detailed)
- Different sentiments (not all positive)
- Timestamps spanning different time periods

**Why?** Real review sections show diversity. A page full of identical 5-star reviews looks fake
and diminishes credibility.

### 3. Use Realistic Review Text

Write review content that sounds natural:

✅ Good: "Great product, exactly as described. Shipping was fast."
❌ Avoid: "This is the most amazing product in the universe!"

Review text should be professional but conversational.

### 4. Respect Character Limits

Different platforms have different limits:
- Amazon: Titles up to 128 characters, reviews up to 10,000 characters
- Google: No strict limit, typically 200–500 characters
- Trustpilot: No limit
- Instagram: Comments limited to visible portion (~125 visible)

Keep content within platform-appropriate lengths.

### 5. Include Context

Show enough context for viewers to understand:

- Product category (if applicable)
- Price point or rating scale
- Number of reviews (social proof metric)
- Time period reviews span

### 6. Use High-Quality Avatars

Download or generate realistic profile pictures for reviewers. Placeholder avatars look unprofessional.

Options:
- Use avatar generator tools (e.g., Gravatar, UI Avatars)
- Find royalty-free images (Unsplash, Pexels)
- Generate AI avatars

### 7. Show Engagement Metrics

Include realistic engagement numbers:

**Amazon:**
- "127 people found this helpful"
- Comment counts

**Google/Trustpilot:**
- Upvote/helpful counts
- Reply counts

**Social Media:**
- Like counts
- Reply counts
- Share counts

Real reviews show social proof through engagement. Include these metrics.

### 8. Test Responsiveness

Show how reviews appear across devices:
- Desktop view
- Tablet view
- Mobile view

Create mockups for all breakpoints to demonstrate responsive design.

❌ **Problem:** Amazon reviews that look like Trustpilot reviews

❌ **Problem:** Reviews with no helpful counts or engagement indicators
✅ **Solution:** Include realistic like counts, helpful indicators, etc.

❌ **Problem:** Reviews only shown on desktop
✅ **Solution:** Create mockups for mobile, tablet, desktop
